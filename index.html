I can tell you this because I wasn't fit enough either. I thought I was just lazy. Turns out, we're not.

So just like you, I gave up. But the mirror reminded me every morning. The weird glances and the cruel laughs were constant reminders. And I know they are for you too.

And the pain? It became a drive I learned to love. Not because Nike solved it, but because each morning I laced up, I saw the reason I bought the shoe. To become THE ATHLETE. And that was enough.

Long-Form Blog · Tech · Career

The 5 Tech Skills Companies Are Hiring For In 2026

Is Artificial Intelligence Replacing The Tech Workforce?

The tech industry is fast evolving with the emergence of Artificial Intelligence, and this evolution is changing the demands of particular skill sets. But just because AI is changing not only the world but the industry that created it, doesn't mean it's going to replace every existing tech skill. 95% of tech companies investing in AI saw zero return from Gen AI.

This means the AI bubble is sagging, and companies are now looking to hire people with particular tech skills that can work with AI, not be replaced by it.

The Tech Skills Companies Are Investing In, That You Should Too

Intermediate tech skills aren't in demand like before because AI is now the intern. Companies don't want to hire people for what AI can do. But there's a catch, they will hire people competent in skills like SQL, because AI can't manage its own data. So the goal isn't just learning tech skills. It's learning the ones AI relies on.

1. SQL

According to Pluralsight, SQL jumped 27% in popularity among tech professionals, making it a solid skill not just for data analysts, but for developers, product managers, and business analysts too. With consistent learning of two hours per day, most beginners grow confident writing queries within a month, and reach proficiency within 3–6 months. Entry-level data analyst roles start between $65K–$75K annually. Senior-level analysts can earn between $100K–$150K. Career path: Data Analytics Intern → Data Analyst → Analytics Manager → Head of Data Analytics.

2. AI System Integration (MCP & Tooling)

Companies are beyond using AI as chatbots that answer questions. They want AI as tools embedded in specific workflows, and Model Context Protocol (MCP) sits at the heart of that. Additional skills include Python, APIs, database management, and LLM knowledge. Expect 6–9 months to build a strong foundation at around 10 hours per week. AI Integration Specialists earn $100K–$170K with a 45% growth rate in the role. Senior AI engineers average $147K, with total compensation above $160K.

3. Cloud Computing

Most AI companies rushed into artificial intelligence without sorting out their cloud foundation, because AI can't be successfully deployed without proper cloud infrastructure. They're now catching up, and so are hospitals, banks, and large organisations across every sector. Demand is outpacing supply. Budget 6–12 months to become job-ready. Cloud engineers in the US earn an average of $130K annually. Senior roles exceed $170K, with top earners in cities like San Francisco reaching $237K. Career path: Junior Cloud Engineer → Cloud Engineer → Cloud Architect → Solutions Architect → Cloud Director.

4. Cybersecurity

AI isn't only being used to solve problems, it's being used to cause havoc, running highly personalised cyber attacks at scale. This positions cybersecurity as one of the most in-demand skills from companies reliant on technology. Experts also predict current encryption will be breakable by 2032, organisations need to start switching to post-quantum cryptography now. Budget 6–12 months to become job-ready. Entry-level roles start at $70K. Senior roles exceed $200K. The median salary reached approximately $120K in 2025, a significant premium over general IT positions averaging $97K. Career path: SOC Analyst → Security Analyst → Penetration Tester → Security Engineer → CISO.

5. Network Engineering

Cloud computing, AI systems, and cybersecurity all sit on top of network infrastructure. None of them exist without solid networking underneath. And with AI-powered cyberattacks rising sharply, companies need network engineers who understand security deeply. Entry-level certifications like CCNA and CompTIA Network+ take 3–6 months of focused study. Advanced certifications like CCNP take 6–12 months. The average US network engineer earns $109K–$110K annually. Top specialists in high-demand markets earn $170K or more. Career path: Junior Network Engineer → Network Administrator → Senior Network Engineer → Network Architect → Director of Infrastructure.

You Shouldn't Pause For A Moment, The Tech Industry Isn't.

It's time you finally learn these skills and build the kind of career you have dreamed of. The moment you commit to one of these skills is the moment you finally become the success you have always wanted. Don't hesitate, because the tech industry won't hesitate to choose efficiency over you.

Choose learning over pausing. The opportunity is here, while the window is open. ↗
